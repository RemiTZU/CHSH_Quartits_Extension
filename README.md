# CHSH Qudits Extension

## Overview

This repository implements an extension of the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) inequality to higher-dimensional quantum systems using **qudits** (4-dimensional quantum systems). While the classical CHSH inequality is formulated for two-level quantum systems (qubits), this project explores the generalization to 4-level quantum systems (qudits) to investigate enhanced quantum correlations and violations of local realism.

## Background

### CHSH Inequality

The CHSH inequality is a fundamental result in quantum mechanics that demonstrates the incompatibility between quantum mechanics and local hidden variable theories. In its original form, it involves:

- Two spatially separated observers (Alice and Bob)
- Each observer can choose between two measurement settings
- Each measurement yields binary outcomes (+1 or -1)
- The CHSH parameter S ≤ 2 for local realistic theories
- Quantum mechanics allows S ≤ 2√2 (Tsirelson's bound)

### Extension to Qudits

This project extends the CHSH framework to **qudits** - quantum systems with d=4 levels (also called quartits). Key aspects of this extension include:

- **Higher-dimensional Hilbert space**: Instead of 2-dimensional qubits, we work with 4-dimensional quantum states
- **Expanded measurement spaces**: More measurement settings and outcomes become available
- **Enhanced quantum correlations**: Potential for stronger violations of classical bounds
- **Novel Bell inequalities**: Investigation of new inequalities suitable for 4-level systems

## Applications

The extension to 4-dimensional systems has several important applications:

- **Quantum cryptography**: Enhanced security protocols using higher-dimensional entanglement
- **Quantum communication**: Increased information capacity per transmitted quantum system
- **Fundamental physics**: Deeper understanding of quantum non-locality in higher dimensions
- **Quantum information processing**: Exploration of computational advantages with qudits

## Project Goals

1. Implement CHSH-type inequalities for 4-dimensional quantum systems
2. Investigate optimal measurement strategies for maximum quantum violation
3. Compare classical and quantum bounds in the qudit setting
4. Explore practical implementations and experimental considerations
